## 2. Purpose

The Jungus Professional Test is designed to measure fine-grained differences within traditional 16-type personality categories. It is based on a second-generation cognitive function model developed by the Jungus team.

The model keeps the classical MBTI and Jungian function vocabulary as a reference point, but extends it by introducing two additional preference dimensions:

- Object-Background preference for perceiving functions.
- Analysis-Holism preference for judging functions.

These dimensions produce 16 Jungus cognitive functions and 64 personality archetypes.

## 3. Theoretical Structure

### 3.1 Object-Background Preference

Object-Background preference describes whether a person tends to perceive the world through relatively independent focal objects or through the surrounding field, context, atmosphere, and background relationships.

This preference applies to perceiving functions and produces:

- Se-O, Se-B
- Si-O, Si-B
- Ne-O, Ne-B
- Ni-O, Ni-B

### 3.2 Analysis-Holism Preference

Analysis-Holism preference describes whether a person tends to make judgments from abstract principles, formal structures, and independent standards, or from situations, relationships, context, and adaptive balance.

This preference applies to judging functions and produces:

- Te-A, Te-H
- Ti-A, Ti-H
- Fe-A, Fe-H
- Fi-A, Fi-H

### 3.3 Sixty-Four Archetypes

Combining the classical 16-type framework with the Object-Background and Analysis-Holism preferences yields 64 personality archetypes.

For example, a traditional INTJ profile may split into multiple archetypal forms depending on how Ni and Te are expressed under the Jungus model.

## 4. Assessment Structure

The Chinese professional test contains a structured item bank and scoring system designed for the Jungus 16-function and 64-archetype model.

The public record discloses high-level structural information and may include the Chinese item text:

- The assessment is in Simplified Chinese.
- The assessment targets cognitive function preferences and type-level archetypes.
- Items are designed to distinguish both traditional cognitive functions and Jungus-specific sub-function variants.
- Results include function-level interpretation and type-level interpretation.
- AI-assisted interpretation may be used in the deployed product, but operational prompts and generation rules are not disclosed in this public record.
